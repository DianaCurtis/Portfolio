About Me

Back-end Developer with M.S. in Mathematics.

Diana Curtis

Back-end Developer

For the past three years I have worked in academia as a graduate teaching associate and as an instructor for mathematics. During those three years, I was fortunate enough to work on research where I was able to expand my programming knowledge and abilities. In a team effort, we created a system of closed differential equations for a predator-prey model where we were then able to generate numerical simulations through MATLAB to visualize the data. After a year and a half, we were able to publish our findings and then later speak at the largest mathematical conference in the United States. Through my research, I realized that I enjoyed creating code that would help me take my mathematics background to a whole new level. Soon I found myself diving into free online classes to help me better develop my programming skills.

After the completion of a couple of courses, I found myself lacking fundamental knowledge and the guidance of a knowledgeable developer. I decided to enroll into an accelerated web development program, LearningFuze, where I spent 12-14 hours a day programming. In the beginning of the program, I worked on the front-end development of multiple applications that allowed me to extend my knowledge of HTML, CSS, JavaScript and jQuery. I was also able to work in a team environment during our first hackathon where we created a Lord of the Rings themed monopoly game. In the last six weeks of the program, I was able to develop my knowledge and skills towards the back-end by collaborating in a final group project. We created the endpoints, through the use of PHP and used SQL to query the database that we built from the ground up. Through this experience, I found that I really enjoyed working in the back-end and look forward to finding a company where I can continue to develop those skills.
